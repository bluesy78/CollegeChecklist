SUNY College Comparison Checklist

Your guide to comparing SUNY Cobleskill and SUNY Oswego

📚

Academic Programs

SUNY Cobleskill
Known for outstanding programs in Agricultural Business, Culinary Arts, and Wildlife Management with a practical, hands-on approach.
SUNY Oswego
Recognized for strong programs in Meteorology, Business, Education and Media studies with 110+ undergraduate and graduate programs.
SUNY Cobleskill
Maintains a smaller 17:1 student-to-faculty ratio, allowing for more personalized instruction and mentoring.
SUNY Oswego
Offers a 17:1 student-to-faculty ratio, balancing individual attention with a more diverse campus community.
SUNY Cobleskill
Emphasizes applied learning with 782-acre working farm, state-of-the-art culinary facilities, and specialized labs.
SUNY Oswego
Features modern facilities like the Shineman Science Center, broadcasting studios, and dedicated internship programs.
SUNY Cobleskill
Strong industry connections in agriculture, food systems, and environmental fields, with high placement rates for technical programs.
SUNY Oswego
Boasts strong alumni network and career services with particularly high placement rates for business, education, and STEM graduates.
🏠

Campus Life

SUNY Cobleskill
Five residence halls with options from traditional dorms to suite-style living in a rural setting with close-knit community feel.
SUNY Oswego
13 residence halls including themed living communities and lakeside residence halls with stunning views of Lake Ontario.
SUNY Cobleskill
Dining services feature farm-to-table options with ingredients from the college's own farm and culinary program influence.
SUNY Oswego
Multiple dining centers across campus with flexible meal plans, special dietary accommodations, and late-night options.
SUNY Cobleskill
Small-town setting with 24/7 campus police, blue light system, and typically low crime statistics.
SUNY Oswego
University Police available 24/7 with comprehensive safety systems, escort services, and emergency notification network.
💰

Financial Considerations

SUNY Cobleskill
Generally has a lower overall cost with affordable tuition and lower cost of living in a rural area.
SUNY Oswego
Standard SUNY tuition rates with higher housing costs but typically more financial aid opportunities available.
SUNY Cobleskill
Specialized scholarships for agriculture, culinary, and technical programs with generous industry support.
SUNY Oswego
Broad scholarship options including Presidential Scholarships worth up to $5,000 annually and specialty program awards.
SUNY Cobleskill
Over 80% of students receive some form of financial aid with significant opportunities for work-study in applied learning settings.
SUNY Oswego
Award-winning financial aid office that processes over $80 million in aid annually with dedicated counseling services.
